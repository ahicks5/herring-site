The Books

The Last City: Age of Darkness

Book One — A Dark Fantasy Epic

Driven by desperation, Elena is on the brink of selling herself to the Den — a grim fate where bodies are commodities for the elite and the privileged few. Despite the pleas of her sister and best friend to explore other options, Elena's panic sends her sprinting toward the Door. In a twist of fate, it swings open, allowing Elena and Bren a taste of freedom in the outside world. However, before they can make it to the forest's protection, they are shot down, dragging them into the clutches of Hell's Keep, beneath the minister's residence.

Meanwhile, Bren finds himself thrust into a perilous alliance with the minister, forced to serve as his assassin to ensure Elena's survival. Faced with the moral quandary of sacrificing his humanity to preserve her life, Bren forms unexpected bonds with fellow assassins, Klaus and Thomas, blurring the lines between friend and foe. As their destinies intertwine, they hurtle toward a pivotal moment where the minister's revelation threatens to reshape their world, leaving them poised on uncertainty.

Unaware of her sister's fate, Alasea struggles with the grim reality of her belief in Elena's and Bren's deaths — all while her mother sits as the minister's captive, awaiting her death on the Red Wall. Broken and alone, Alasea embarks on a quest for survival, guided by her mother's cryptic mention of a Book that holds the key to their family's history and the city's blood-woven past.

The Author

Meet Chrislyn

Chrislyn R. Herring

Chrislyn R. Herring hails from a small town in South Texas, where her passion for writing and storytelling began. Drawing inspiration from literary giants like J.R.R. Tolkien and contemporary masters such as Sarah J. Maas, she continued on her writing journey with a degree in English Literature from Texas A&M University. Today, she resides and crafts her stories in the vibrant city of Austin, Texas, alongside her friends and family, where every day brings new inspiration and creativity to her work. When she is not writing in coffee shops, Chrislyn spends most of her time in the climbing gym, trying new cuisine, and out in nature.
